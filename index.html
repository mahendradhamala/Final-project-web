About Sudurpashchim Province

Sudurpashchim Province, meaning "Far-Western Province," is one of Nepal's seven federal provinces. Located in the westernmost part of the country, it borders India to the west and south, and China's Tibet Autonomous Region to the north.

The province covers an area of 19,539 km² and has a population of approximately 2.7 million people. It consists of nine districts, each offering unique cultural and natural attractions.

Sudurpashchim is known for its diverse landscapes ranging from the Himalayan peaks in the north to the Terai plains in the south, with beautiful mid-hill regions in between. The region is rich in biodiversity, cultural heritage, and offers authentic experiences away from mainstream tourism.

Top Destinations

Khaptad National Park

Explore this beautiful mid-mountain national park with its rich biodiversity, alpine grasslands, and stunning views of the Himalayas.

Badimalika Temple

This sacred Hindu temple located in Bajura district is an important pilgrimage site with breathtaking mountain views.

Shuklaphanta Wildlife Reserve

Home to endangered species like the Bengal tiger and one-horned rhinoceros, this reserve offers excellent wildlife viewing.

Api Himal

For adventure seekers, the Api Himal region offers challenging trekking routes with spectacular mountain scenery.
